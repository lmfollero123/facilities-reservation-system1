ABSTRACT
As the hospitality industry continues to change, hotels and restaurants are now turning to technology to improve the way they operate and serve their guests. Today, people want services that are fast and secure and are modern, which pushes hotels to find smarter solutions for managing daily operations. At the same time, challenges remain such as handling room reservations, keeping guest data safe, securing payments, and reducing delay in service. To address these, this study presents SOLIERA: Hotel and Restaurant Management System with Gemini Room Reservation and RFID Door Lock Integration, a system built to improve guest experience while making hotel operations more efficient.

Statement of the Problem
In today’s hospitality industry, hotels and restaurants require fast, reliable, and integrated systems to manage daily operations and meet guest expectations. However, many establishments still rely on traditional, manual processes such as paper logs, printed receipts, manual inventory tracking, and walkie-talkie communication. These outdated methods often result in delays, errors, and miscommunication among staff.

Project Objectives
This study aims to design and develop SOLIERA, an integrated hotel and restaurant management system that enhances operational efficiency and delivers a modern, secure, and user-friendly experience. The system integrates AI-powered room reservations and RFID door lock technology to automate core hotel operations and improve guest satisfaction. The specific objectives of the study are to:
	Develop SOLIERA-Core1 – Implement the core operational modules of the system.
	Automate front desk processes – Facilitate efficient guest check-in, check-out, and room reservations.


Scope and Limitations
The study focuses on designing and implementing SOLIERA: Hotel and Restaurant Management System with Gemini Room Reservation and RFID Door Lock Integration, emphasizing CORE1 modules for daily hotel operations. 
Scope
	System Modules – The study includes the development of core modules such as:
	Front Desk and Reception
	Reservation and Booking
	Target Users – Hotel staff (front desk, housekeeping, admin) and guests using AI-assisted booking and keyless access.
	Technology Integration – Combines AI for personalized reservations with RFID door locks for security and convenience.
	Operational Environment – Designed for mid-sized hotels and restaurants needing digital transformation.
Limitations
	System Scope – The project focuses on the CORE1 modules and does not cover advanced analytics, third-party integrations beyond popular booking platforms (Agoda, Booking.com), or mobile application development at this stage.
	Hardware Dependencies – RFID door locks require compatible Arduino and ESP32 devices.
These limitations define realistic boundaries, ensuring SOLIERA achieves automation, guest satisfaction, and secure hotel management.

Ethical, Legal, and Security Considerations
The development of SOLIERA followed ethical, legal, and security principles to ensure responsible, safe, and compliant use of data and technology.
Ethical Considerations
	Informed Consent – Participants were briefed on the study’s purpose, data use, and their right to withdraw.

	Confidentiality– Guest and staff data was kept secure and anonymized in reports.
	Transparency – Users were informed about AI recommendations, automated processes, and RFID access.
	Responsible AI Use – Gemini AI assists decisions without overriding human judgment, ensuring ethical use.
Legal Considerations
	Data Privacy Compliance – Ensures secure handling and storage of personal and payment information.
	Intellectual Property – Development follows copyright and licensing rules for software and third-party libraries.
	User Agreements – Hotel staff and guests accept terms outlining responsibilities, limitations, and proper system use.

Security Considerations

	RFID Access Security – RFID door lock integration ensures secure, keyless access while logging all entry and exit events for monitoring purposes.
	Data Encryption – Sensitive guest and transaction data are encrypted.
	User Authentication – Secure login with role-based access control.
	System Monitoring and Backup – Regular backups, activity monitoring, and intrusion detection.
	Physical Security – Hardware like RFID readers, Arduino, and servers are protected from tampering or theft.

Statistical Treatment of Data 

To evaluate the effectiveness and usability of the SOLIERA system, quantitative survey responses were analyzed using weighted mean, percentage distribution, and comparative analysis.

Weighted Mean Formula
The weighted mean was used to measure the average response for Likert-scale questions (1–5 scale).
"Weighted Mean (WM)"=(∑(f×x))/N

Where:
	f = frequency of responses
	x = Likert scale value
	N = total number of respondents

DISCUSSION
The discussion interprets the results and connects them to the study’s objectives and broader industry implications:
Operational Impact – SOLIERA streamlined hotel operations by integrating reservations, billing, inventory, and housekeeping into a single platform. The reduction in manual tasks minimized human errors and improved coordination between departments.
Technological Integration – AI-powered room recommendations and RFID door locks enhanced service personalization and security, demonstrating the potential of emerging technologies in hospitality management.
Comparison with Existing Systems – Unlike traditional POS and manual workflows, SOLIERA offers real-time updates, mobile access, automated billing, and integration with modern devices, addressing previously identified inefficiencies.
Limitations and Observations – Performance depends on accurate input data and stable internet connectivity. User training is essential to maximize system benefits.
The discussion highlights how SOLIERA aligns with current trends in digital transformation within the hospitality industry and supports future scalability.





CONCLUSION
SOLIERA: Hotel and Restaurant Management System with Gemini Room Reservation and RFID Door Lock Integration successfully integrates AI-driven reservation and RFID-based security to enhance hotel operations. The system demonstrated improved operational efficiency, reduced manual errors, and provided a more personalized, secure experience for guests. CORE1 modules effectively addressed the key challenges faced by hotels, including front desk automation, reservation management, billing, inventory tracking, and housekeeping coordination.

Overall, the study confirms that intelligent, automated, and integrated systems can significantly improve both staff performance and guest satisfaction in modern hospitality management.
RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the findings of this study, the following recommendations are proposed:
System Expansion – Future development should include mobile applications, advanced analytics, and full integration with all third-party booking platforms.
Staff Training – Continuous training programs should be conducted to ensure efficient use of AI and RFID features.
Hardware Optimization – Hotels should maintain compatible and updated RFID hardware to ensure seamless operation.
Regular Updates and Maintenance – Periodic system updates and security patches are necessary to maintain performance and data protection.
Scalability – Consider extending the system to multi-branch hotel operations or integrating additional modules such as loyalty programs, dynamic pricing, and marketing automation.
