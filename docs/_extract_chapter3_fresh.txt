Chapter 1 
INTRODUCTION 
1.1 Background of the Capstone Project
The Barangays in the Philippines serve as the smallest administrative units responsible for delivering basic public services and managing shared community resources. Among these responsibilities is the proper scheduling and allocation of public facilities such as basketball courts, playgrounds, health care rooms, and barangay-owned equipment like chairs and tables. Despite the importance of these services, many barangays continue to rely on manual and informal processes for managing reservations and resource usage.
In Barangay Culiat, Quezon City, the reservation of public facilities is commonly done through personal visits to the barangay hall or informal communication channels such as private messages on social media platforms. These methods often result in delayed responses, overlooked reservation requests, unclear scheduling, and frequent conflicts due to the absence of a centralized and organized reservation system. Furthermore, manual record-keeping increases the risk of inaccurate data, misplaced resources, and inefficient coordination among barangay personnel.
To address these challenges, the AI-Driven Facilities Reservation System with Predictive Scheduling Features was conceptualized and developed. The system aims to modernize the reservation process by providing a centralized digital platform that enables residents to check real-time availability, submit reservation requests online, and receive timely updates. By incorporating intelligent scheduling and automation, the system seeks to improve operational efficiency, transparency, and accessibility in barangay facility management.
1.2 Context and Scope
This capstone project addresses the challenges of barangay-level public service management, focusing on the digital transformation of facility and equipment reservation processes. The system is designed as a web-based application accessible to residents, barangay staff, and administrators with defined roles and permissions.
The scope of the system includes:
• Online reservation of barangay facilities and equipment• Real-time availability and scheduling display• Automated and AI-driven predictive scheduling to reduce conflicts• Centralized reservation records and monitoring• Communication support between residents and barangay personnel
The system is intended for use within Barangay Culiat and is designed to support current operational needs while remaining scalable for future enhancements. Features such as online payments and mobile application support are considered beyond the scope of the current implementation.
1.3 Problem Statement
1.3.1 Main Problem
Barangay Culiat experiences inefficiencies in managing and scheduling its community facilities due to reliance on manual and informal reservation procedures. These practices result in delayed communication, inaccurate records, scheduling conflicts, and limited accessibility for residents.
1.3.2 Specific Problems
The following specific problems were identified:
1. Lack of a centralized system for real-time monitoring of facility and equipment availability
2. Frequent double bookings and scheduling conflicts
3. Delayed responses or overlooked requests caused by manual and unorganized reservation methods
4. Difficulty in tracking barangay equipment, leading to misplaced or inaccurately recorded resources
5. Inconvenient reservation process that requires physical visits or reliance on social media communication
1.4 Objectives and Goals
1.4.1 General Objective
The general objective of this project is to develop an AI-driven reservation and scheduling system that improves the efficiency, accuracy, and accessibility of facility and equipment management in Barangay Culiat.
1.4.2 Specific Objectives
The project aims to:
	Develop a digital platform for real-time viewing and reservation of barangay facilities and equipment
	Implement AI-based predictive scheduling to minimize double bookings and scheduling conflicts
	Provide an efficient communication channel between residents and barangay staff
	Improve monitoring and tracking of facility usage and barangay-owned equipment
	Enhance the overall quality and reliability of public service delivery in Barangay Culiat
1.4.3 Goal
Modernize Barangay Culiat’s facility and equipment management by introducing a centralized, automated, and intelligent reservation system that increases transparency, convenience, and operational efficiency for both residents and barangay personnel.
1.4.4 Users and Beneficiaries
	Users: Residents of Barangay Culiat, barangay staff, and system administrators.
	Beneficiaries: The entire community of Barangay Culiat, as the system reduces scheduling conflicts, improves access to facilities, and enhances overall public service delivery.
1.5 Significance and Relevance
This system provides a modern and organized approach to managing barangay facility reservations. It enables barangay officials to efficiently allocate resources, reduce administrative workload, and maintain accurate records. For residents, the system offers a more convenient, transparent, and accessible reservation process.
This study is significant as it demonstrates how small local government units can adopt digital and AI-driven solutions to improve operational efficiency and public service delivery. It also serves as a practical reference for future information systems designed for community-level governance and resource management.

2.1 Agile Scrum Methodology Overview
Agile Scrum is a software development methodology that emphasizes iterative progress, collaboration, and adaptability. Projects are divided into short cycles called sprints, allowing teams to deliver functional features quickly and adjust based on user feedback. Scrum roles, including Product Owner, Scrum Master, and Development Team, ensure organized workflow and accountability. In the context of the AI-Driven Facilities Reservation System for Barangay Culiat, Agile Scrum facilitates rapid development of features such as real-time facility availability, predictive scheduling, and user communication. This approach ensures that the system can adapt to the needs of residents and barangay staff efficiently, minimizing delays and improving service delivery.
2.2 Enterprise Architecture Concepts
Enterprise Architecture is a structured framework that defines how an organization’s processes, information systems, and technology work together. It serves as a blueprint, ensuring that all components are connected, efficient, and aligned with organizational goals.
In simple terms, EA is like a map of the system: it shows how different parts of the organization interact and how new systems can fit without causing problems.
For the AI-Driven Facilities Reservation System in Barangay Culiat, EA ensures that the system integrates smoothly with existing barangay operations. This includes facility scheduling, equipment tracking, and communication between residents and staff. Following EA principles also allows the system to be scalable, maintainable, and adaptable to future digital improvements. 2.5 Relevant Studies and Research
Several existing systems have demonstrated the benefits of digital reservation and appointment technologies, particularly in improving resource management and user accessibility. For instance, the AI-Driven Sit-in Reservation System at the University of Cebu optimizes resource allocation, provides real-time monitoring, and offers a user-friendly interface, although it is limited to educational laboratories and sit-in reservations. Similarly, web-based appointment systems at Mindanao State University and PRC IV-A improve scheduling efficiency and operational workflow, but they either lack AI-based predictive features or are constrained to specific office or professional services, with occasional accessibility issues. The Customer-Driven Facility Management System at Mapua University incorporates user requirements and automates reservation processes, yet it does not include predictive scheduling and focuses solely on university settings. Broader analyses, such as studies on AI adoption in local governments and optimization techniques for appointment scheduling, provide valuable insights into efficiency gains and algorithmic approaches, but they are either too general or healthcare-focused, with limited practical guidance for community facility management. Building on these findings, the proposed AI-Driven Facilities Reservation System for Barangay Culiat aims to integrate the strengths of these existing solutions while addressing their limitations. By implementing AI-driven predictive scheduling, real-time notifications, mobile and web accessibility, and optimization algorithms to prevent double bookings, the system enhances community-wide facility management, streamlines communication between residents and barangay staff, and ensures organized, transparent, and efficient use of barangay resources.

3.7 Integration Approach for Information Systems

3.8 Introduction to TOGAF and the Four Architectural 	Domains
Enterprise Architecture:Goal: Provides a comprehensive view of organizational processes, resources, information flows, and systems, ensuring alignment between business needs and technology infrastructure.
Relevance to Barangay Facilities Management:Facilitates a unified understanding of how barangay facilities are scheduled, monitored, and maintained. It ensures that AI-driven reservation processes align with operational workflows and community requirements, helping barangay staff manage resources efficiently while providing transparency and accountability to residents.
Architecture for Applications:Goal: Defines the structure and integration of software applications to meet functional requirements, ensure system reliability, and support future scalability.
Relevance to Barangay Facilities Management:Supports AI-driven reservation and predictive scheduling applications that optimize facility usage. Applications can handle real-time updates, prevent double bookings, and provide a user-friendly interface for residents, ensuring smooth coordination between the community and barangay staff.
Technology Framework:Goal: Specifies the hardware, networks, and technological standards required to support applications and enterprise processes reliably and securely.
Relevance to Barangay Facilities Management:Ensures the system leverages cloud-based servers, secure mobile and web platforms, and AI processing units to provide fast, reliable, and accessible services to residents. The framework supports scalability as demand for facility reservations grows, enabling uninterrupted operations during peak periods.
Compliance with Social Service and Community Goals:
Enhance Accessibility: Residents can easily reserve barangay facilities anytime via web or mobile platforms, reducing bottlenecks in community service access.
Efficient Resource Management: AI-driven predictive algorithms optimize scheduling to maximize facility utilization while avoiding conflicts.
Data Driven Decision Making: Usage data from the system informs barangay staff about peak hours, popular facilities, and maintenance needs, supporting evidence-based planning and policy adjustments.
Advantages of TOGAF:
Proactive Risk Management: Early identification of potential scheduling conflicts, system failures, or resource shortages allows mitigation before they impact residents.
Scalable and Flexible Architecture: Supports system growth as the community expands or additional facilities are added, with minimal disruption.
Interoperability and Integration: Ensures the reservation system can communicate with other barangay platforms (e.g., announcements, billing, or community portals), providing a seamless user experience.
